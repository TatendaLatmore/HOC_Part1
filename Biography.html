Biography

Alan Turing (1912-1954) was a pioneering mathematician, computer scientist, and codebreaker. Turing displayed exceptional mathematical talent from a young age. He made significant contributions to mathematical logic, including the development of the concept of computability and the Church-Turing thesis, which laid the theoretical groundwork for computer science.
